The San Diego housing market at a glance

The typical home in San Diego is worth about $1,007,800 (Zillow ZHVI, 2026-05) — about 30% above the California typical ($775,550), and about 185% above the national typical ($354,135). That makes it the 2nd most expensive of the 16 launch cities we cover. Entry-level homes start near $660,466, while higher-budget homes reach about $1,744,381. Condos and co-ops are more affordable here at a typical $644,475 — about 79% below the $1,154,746 typical single-family home. Bedroom counts range from $487,056 (1-bed) to $1,981,026 (5+-bed), with the biggest step up from a 1- to 2-bedroom home (about +51.86%).

Frequently asked questions

How much is a typical home in San Diego?

As of 2026-05, the typical home value in San Diego, CA is about $1,007,800 (Zillow Research ZHVI) — about 30% above the California typical ($775,550). Starter homes are around $660,466 and higher-budget homes around $1,744,381.

What is the monthly mortgage payment on a typical San Diego home?

With 20% down at 6.5% over 30 years, the estimated full payment (principal, interest, California property tax at 0.71% and insurance) on a $1,007,800 home is about $5,986/mo. Adjust the numbers in the calculator above.

What income do I need to buy a home in San Diego?

Using a typical $1,007,800 home, 20% down and a 28% housing-cost-to-income guideline, you'd want roughly $256,543 in annual household income. A planning estimate, not a lending decision.

Is it cheaper to rent or buy in San Diego?

Typical rent is about $2,987/mo versus an estimated $5,986/mo to own a typical home with 20% down — a difference of $2,999/mo. This ignores maintenance, appreciation, your time horizon and transaction costs, so treat it as a snapshot, not a decision.

Are these San Diego figures exact?

No. Home values and rent are measured Zillow Research data; payments, income, taxes, PMI and closing costs are calculated estimates. City values may not match a specific listing, appraisal or lender valuation.
