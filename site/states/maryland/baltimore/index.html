The Baltimore housing market at a glance

The typical home in Baltimore is worth about $191,986 (Zillow ZHVI, 2026-05) — about 56% below the Maryland typical ($434,033), and about 46% below the national typical ($354,135). That makes it the 16th most expensive of the 16 launch cities we cover. Entry-level homes start near $102,902, while higher-budget homes reach about $340,952. Unusually, condos ($219,830) run higher than single-family homes ($189,881) in this market. Bedroom counts range from $156,234 (1-bed) to $388,952 (5+-bed), with the biggest step up from a 4- to 5+-bedroom home (about +37.33%).

Frequently asked questions

How much is a typical home in Baltimore?

As of 2026-05, the typical home value in Baltimore, MD is about $191,986 (Zillow Research ZHVI) — about 56% below the Maryland typical ($434,033). Starter homes are around $102,902 and higher-budget homes around $340,952.

What is the monthly mortgage payment on a typical Baltimore home?

With 20% down at 6.5% over 30 years, the estimated full payment (principal, interest, Maryland property tax at 1.05% and insurance) on a $191,986 home is about $1,195/mo. Adjust the numbers in the calculator above.

What income do I need to buy a home in Baltimore?

Using a typical $191,986 home, 20% down and a 28% housing-cost-to-income guideline, you'd want roughly $51,214 in annual household income. A planning estimate, not a lending decision.

Is it cheaper to rent or buy in Baltimore?

Typical rent is about $1,786/mo versus an estimated $1,195/mo to own a typical home with 20% down — a difference of $591/mo. This ignores maintenance, appreciation, your time horizon and transaction costs, so treat it as a snapshot, not a decision.

Are these Baltimore figures exact?

No. Home values and rent are measured Zillow Research data; payments, income, taxes, PMI and closing costs are calculated estimates. City values may not match a specific listing, appraisal or lender valuation.
