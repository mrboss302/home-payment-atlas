The Philadelphia housing market at a glance

The typical home in Philadelphia is worth about $236,883 (Zillow ZHVI, 2026-05) — about 18% below the Pennsylvania typical ($289,277), and about 33% below the national typical ($354,135). That makes it the 15th most expensive of the 16 launch cities we cover. Entry-level homes start near $138,660, while higher-budget homes reach about $412,667. Unusually, condos ($290,117) run higher than single-family homes ($230,445) in this market. Bedroom counts range from $266,567 (1-bed) to $446,942 (5+-bed), with the biggest step up from a 3- to 4-bedroom home (about +49.56%).

Frequently asked questions

How much is a typical home in Philadelphia?

As of 2026-05, the typical home value in Philadelphia, PA is about $236,883 (Zillow Research ZHVI) — about 18% below the Pennsylvania typical ($289,277). Starter homes are around $138,660 and higher-budget homes around $412,667.

What is the monthly mortgage payment on a typical Philadelphia home?

With 20% down at 6.5% over 30 years, the estimated full payment (principal, interest, Pennsylvania property tax at 1.49% and insurance) on a $236,883 home is about $1,561/mo. Adjust the numbers in the calculator above.

What income do I need to buy a home in Philadelphia?

Using a typical $236,883 home, 20% down and a 28% housing-cost-to-income guideline, you'd want roughly $66,900 in annual household income. A planning estimate, not a lending decision.

Is it cheaper to rent or buy in Philadelphia?

Typical rent is about $1,806/mo versus an estimated $1,561/mo to own a typical home with 20% down — a difference of $245/mo. This ignores maintenance, appreciation, your time horizon and transaction costs, so treat it as a snapshot, not a decision.

Are these Philadelphia figures exact?

No. Home values and rent are measured Zillow Research data; payments, income, taxes, PMI and closing costs are calculated estimates. City values may not match a specific listing, appraisal or lender valuation.
