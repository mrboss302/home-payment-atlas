The Dallas housing market at a glance

The typical home in Dallas is worth about $312,024 (Zillow ZHVI, 2026-05) — about the same as the Texas typical ($302,550), and about 12% below the national typical ($354,135). That makes it the 13th most expensive of the 16 launch cities we cover. Entry-level homes start near $189,527, while higher-budget homes reach about $779,391. Condos and co-ops are more affordable here at a typical $213,446 — about 53% below the $326,854 typical single-family home. Bedroom counts range from $146,055 (1-bed) to $1,400,016 (5+-bed), with the biggest step up from a 4- to 5+-bedroom home (about +128.25%).

Frequently asked questions

How much is a typical home in Dallas?

As of 2026-05, the typical home value in Dallas, TX is about $312,024 (Zillow Research ZHVI) — about the same as the Texas typical ($302,550). Starter homes are around $189,527 and higher-budget homes around $779,391.

What is the monthly mortgage payment on a typical Dallas home?

With 20% down at 6.5% over 30 years, the estimated full payment (principal, interest, Texas property tax at 1.68% and insurance) on a $312,024 home is about $2,106/mo. Adjust the numbers in the calculator above.

What income do I need to buy a home in Dallas?

Using a typical $312,024 home, 20% down and a 28% housing-cost-to-income guideline, you'd want roughly $90,257 in annual household income. A planning estimate, not a lending decision.

Is it cheaper to rent or buy in Dallas?

Typical rent is about $1,648/mo versus an estimated $2,106/mo to own a typical home with 20% down — a difference of $458/mo. This ignores maintenance, appreciation, your time horizon and transaction costs, so treat it as a snapshot, not a decision.

Are these Dallas figures exact?

No. Home values and rent are measured Zillow Research data; payments, income, taxes, PMI and closing costs are calculated estimates. City values may not match a specific listing, appraisal or lender valuation.
