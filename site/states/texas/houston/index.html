The Houston housing market at a glance

The typical home in Houston is worth about $264,952 (Zillow ZHVI, 2026-05) — about 12% below the Texas typical ($302,550), and about 25% below the national typical ($354,135). That makes it the 14th most expensive of the 16 launch cities we cover. Entry-level homes start near $178,046, while higher-budget homes reach about $498,660. Condos and co-ops are more affordable here at a typical $134,271 — about 102% below the $271,206 typical single-family home. Bedroom counts range from $114,410 (1-bed) to $567,729 (5+-bed), with the biggest step up from a 4- to 5+-bedroom home (about +71.28%).

Frequently asked questions

How much is a typical home in Houston?

As of 2026-05, the typical home value in Houston, TX is about $264,952 (Zillow Research ZHVI) — about 12% below the Texas typical ($302,550). Starter homes are around $178,046 and higher-budget homes around $498,660.

What is the monthly mortgage payment on a typical Houston home?

With 20% down at 6.5% over 30 years, the estimated full payment (principal, interest, Texas property tax at 1.68% and insurance) on a $264,952 home is about $1,788/mo. Adjust the numbers in the calculator above.

What income do I need to buy a home in Houston?

Using a typical $264,952 home, 20% down and a 28% housing-cost-to-income guideline, you'd want roughly $76,629 in annual household income. A planning estimate, not a lending decision.

Is it cheaper to rent or buy in Houston?

Typical rent is about $1,566/mo versus an estimated $1,788/mo to own a typical home with 20% down — a difference of $222/mo. This ignores maintenance, appreciation, your time horizon and transaction costs, so treat it as a snapshot, not a decision.

Are these Houston figures exact?

No. Home values and rent are measured Zillow Research data; payments, income, taxes, PMI and closing costs are calculated estimates. City values may not match a specific listing, appraisal or lender valuation.
