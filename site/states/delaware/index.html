The Delaware housing market at a glance

In Delaware, the typical home is worth about $410,212 (Zillow ZHVI, 2026-05) — about 16% above the national typical value of $354,135, making it an above-average housing market and the 21st most expensive of 51 states and DC we track. Entry-level homes start near $278,545, while higher-budget homes reach about $643,757. Condos and co-ops are more accessible at a typical $418,696, roughly -2% less than the $409,755 typical single-family home. By size, a 2-bedroom runs about $283,172, a 3-bedroom runs about $370,320, a 4-bedroom runs about $522,665.

Renting vs buying in Delaware

Owning a typical Delaware home costs an estimated $2,389/mo with 20% down (principal, interest, taxes and insurance), plus upfront cash — the down payment and roughly 3% in closing costs. A fair rent-vs-buy comparison weighs that against local rent and also accounts for maintenance, how long you'll stay, the opportunity cost of your down payment, expected home-price appreciation, and the transaction costs on both sides.

Use the calculator above to plan the buying side, then compare it against rents you're seeing locally.

How this Delaware calculator works

Where the home values come from. The prices on this page — typical, starter, higher-budget, single-family, condo and by-bedroom — are measured data from Zillow Research Home Value Index (ZHVI), a smoothed, seasonally adjusted estimate of typical values. We use the latest available month (2026-05): the middle price tier for "typical," the lower tier for "starter," and the upper tier for "higher-budget."

Where the payments come from. Monthly payments are calculated by this tool with a standard fixed-rate formula and editable assumptions: 6.5% rate, 30-year term, Delaware property tax of 0.57%, 0.35% insurance, and 0.5% PMI when down payment is under 20%. They are estimates, not quotes. See the full methodology.

Frequently asked questions

How much is a typical home in Delaware?

As of 2026-05, the typical home value in Delaware is about $410,212, based on Zillow Research (ZHVI) data — about 16% above the national typical value of $354,135. Starter homes are around $278,545 and higher-budget homes around $643,757.

What income do I need to buy a home in Delaware?

Using a typical $410,212 home, 20% down, Delaware's property-tax assumption of 0.57%, and a 28% housing-cost-to-income guideline, you'd want roughly $102,386 in annual household income. This is a planning estimate, not a lending decision — lenders weigh credit, debts and other factors.

What would the monthly payment be on a typical Delaware home?

With 20% down at 6.5% over 30 years, principal and interest on a $410,212 home is about $2,074. Adding estimated Delaware taxes, insurance and (under 20% down) PMI brings the full payment to roughly $2,389 — use the calculator above to fine-tune it.

How are Delaware property taxes handled?

The calculator prefills Delaware with an approximate effective property-tax rate of 0.57% of home value per year, plus a 0.35% insurance assumption. Rates vary widely by county and city, so entering your own annual dollar amounts gives the most accurate result.

Are these Delaware home values current?

They reflect the latest available Zillow ZHVI month (2026-05) and are smoothed, seasonally adjusted estimates. They may not match a specific listing, appraisal or lender valuation.
